Task 2. Modular Architecture Design (25 pts)

Scenario: Your team is designing a robot for an FTC-style game. The robot must: (1) drive across a field, (2) pick up 10 cm foam cubes from the floor, (3) deposit cubes on a shelf 18 inches high, and (4) deploy a flag at the end of the match. The robot starts within an 18"×18"×18" box.

Your task: Design a modular architecture for this robot — identify at least five modules, define each module's job, specify how it interfaces with the rest of the system, and draw the architecture diagram. Then evaluate your architecture against two modular design properties.

Grading criteria — Task 2
  • Five modules identified, each with a clearly stated job — 5 pts
  • Interface table: connection type and physical mechanism for each module pair — 6 pts
  • Architecture diagram: all modules as labeled boxes, all interfaces as labeled lines, central integration module clearly identified — 8 pts
  • Modular property evaluation: two properties cited with specific evidence from this design — 4 pts
  • Trade-off analysis: one limitation of the proposed architecture explained — 2 pts
